Design project to build social cohesion

Exploring social support for university students mentored by ThinkPlace’s Leone Murphy

Challenge
Social integration is one aspect which impacts a student’s learning experience, and a number of indicators suggest that AUT could be supporting students social needs more. The aim of this case study was to increase understanding of what social support looks like for vulnerable Pacifica students at AUT University. 
I was tasked with leading a student team to identify opportunities for AUT as a university to foster social support, and prototype one solution for first year students.
Approach
This was a co-design project.  Using ThinkPlace’s four voices model to augment the design process allowed me to find the intersection of the needs of a large organisation and the users. I wanted to ensure we could learn from the people already providing social support to students as an additional form of expertise. 
I guided our team through the research process using scheduled and intercept interviews, data analysis, an ideation workshop, iterative prototyping, report writing, and presentation of results.
Impact
This research project was successful in using design thinking methods to generate new and useful user insights, helping build a picture of the unmet needs for Pacifica students. As the idea has been tested with students, both the strengths and areas of caution were known and validated. 
The resulting report was concise, with actionable insights.  The head of department was fully briefed to allow for effective and inclusive decision making in this area.
